## SOURCING QUICK REFERENCE

### Primary Parts Sources

**RockAuto.com**
- Pros: Huge selection, competitive prices, multiple quality tiers
- Cons: Shipping can be expensive, multiple warehouses = multiple shipments
- Best for: Brake parts, suspension, steering, engine parts, electrical
- Tip: Use catalog to get exact part numbers, then compare prices elsewhere

**NAPA Auto Parts**
- Pros: Local availability, easy returns, knowledgeable staff
- Cons: Higher prices than online
- Best for: Emergency parts, fluids, filters, belts, hoses
- Tip: Sign up for commercial account for discounts

**O'Reilly / AutoZone / Advance Auto**
- Pros: Local, convenient, tool loan program
- Cons: Variable prices, limited specialty parts
- Best for: Same-day needs, fluids, batteries, common wear items
- Tip: Check online for coupons and price matching

**ComancheClub.com**
- Pros: MJ-specific knowledge, used parts marketplace
- Cons: Individual sellers, condition varies
- Best for: MJ-specific parts, hard-to-find items, technical advice
- Tip: Search forums before buying new - someone may have used parts

**Local Junkyards / Pull-A-Part**
- Pros: Cheap, can inspect before buying, XJ parts interchange
- Cons: Time-consuming, condition unknown until tested
- Best for: XJ interchange parts, interior pieces, body panels, complete assemblies
- Tip: Bring tools, take photos/measurements, test electrical parts before leaving

**Amazon**
- Pros: Fast shipping (Prime), easy returns, reviews
- Cons: Quality varies wildly by seller, some knockoffs
- Best for: Tools, supplies, generic parts, aftermarket accessories
- Tip: Check seller ratings carefully, compare part numbers

**Facebook Marketplace / Craigslist**
- Pros: Local pickup, no shipping, negotiable
- Cons: Must inspect carefully, scams possible
- Best for: Complete assemblies (transmission, transfer case, axles), larger items
- Tip: Meet in public, bring cash, inspect before paying

**Quadratec / Morris 4x4 (out of business) / 4 Wheel Parts**
- Pros: Jeep specialists, quality parts, good tech support
- Cons: Higher prices
- Best for: Lift components, off-road upgrades, hard-to-find MJ parts
- Tip: Watch for sales, free shipping promotions

## SHOPPING STRATEGY

### Bulk Ordering
Order as much as possible in single shipments to save on shipping:
1. Create Phase 1 shopping list after Phase 0 assessment
2. Order all Phase 1 parts at once from RockAuto
3. Order fluids/consumables locally
4. Order Phase 2 parts when Phase 1 is half complete

### When to Buy New vs. Used

**ALWAYS NEW:**
- Brake pads/shoes, rotors/drums
- Brake hydraulics (wheel cylinders, calipers, master cylinder)
- Steering components (tie rods, ball joints)
- Gaskets and seals
- Filters
- Fluids
- Spark plugs, ignition components

**ACCEPTABLE USED:**
- Gauge clusters (test before buying)
- Switches (test before buying)
- Interior trim pieces
- Doors and glass
- Complete assemblies (transmission, transfer case) if low miles and tested
- Driveshafts (inspect carefully for cracks/damage)

**JUNKYARD BEST:**
- Window regulators
- Interior panels
- Trim pieces
- Mirrors
- Light housings
- HVAC components
